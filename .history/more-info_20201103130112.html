    1999- 2000 (300 employees)

    The Situation

    CF made a loss for the first in their history. They became profitable within 6 months after our direct intervention. 
    It was found that the board of directors was not even aware that CF was making a loss.
    At the same time CF was caught up in labour court cases, which they lost.
    Financiers froze all access to funding. 
    CF had the worst product quality ever, which resulted in claims in the US, Taiwan and China of USD 15m. Products had to be recalled on a continuous basis. 
    The financial department accidentally lost their debtors information. They did not know who owed them, aging and how much they were owed.
    An international shareholder had to sell their shares urgently to comply with share requirements of overseas equity markets.
    Morale plunged, affecting management and all other employees. 
    Equipment was old, obsolete and poorly maintained.
    The company accidentally gave away their brand at no cost to their representative in the East.
    The company produced 132 different flavours that made quality control impossible.

    Rescue

    We settled favourable terms with creditors and the banks. 
    CF finally sold a shareholder’s shares to another food company. This enabled the company to have enough cash for operations, expenses and upgrades. 
    We negotiated with claimants in US, Canada, Iowa and Hong Kong, reducing claims substantially.
    Management and risk control systems was implemented.
    Reduced tension with the union and provided comfort to the Union. 
    Replaced old technology and provided training to every employee.
    We held weekly meetings with shareholders and stakeholders to grow everyone's support.
